Key Differentiator

The Contextual Audit Engine:
Verify Claims, Not Just Numbers

Most tools check if the numbers add up. VBF Syndic Pro+ goes further: it helps the commissaire aux comptes assess whether management decisions reflect the best use of community funds — by providing the data needed to ask the right questions.

📋 Contract Renewal Verification

The syndic announces: “We changed the gardening contractor — the new one costs less.” But is that the full picture?

The contextual audit engine allows the commissaire to compare the old contract against the new one side by side: What was the previous scope of service? What is the new scope? Is the cost per intervention actually lower, or was the number of interventions reduced? The commissaire can verify whether the community is paying less for the same quality, or simply receiving less service.

💶 Cost Reduction Claims

The syndic states: “Security costs will be lower next year.” The commissaire needs to verify: is this a genuine efficiency gain?

With the audit engine, the commissaire can cross-reference the current security contract, past invoices, and the proposed new terms. Did the number of guard hours decrease? Were overnight shifts removed? Is the quoted rate genuinely competitive, or does the reduction come from cutting coverage? The data is structured for this exact type of analysis.

🧾 Invoice-Level Traceability

Every charge recorded in the system can be traced back to its source document. The commissaire can verify each expense individually.

The audit engine flags entries that lack supporting documentation, detects duplicate charges, identifies amounts that fall outside expected ranges for a given account category, and highlights supplier changes mid-year. This gives the commissaire a structured checklist to work through, rather than manually reviewing stacks of paper.

📊 Budget vs. Actual Analysis

Owners approved a budget at the last AG. Did the syndic stay within those limits? Where did variances occur, and are they justified?

The contextual audit automatically compares budgeted amounts against actual expenses, line by line and category by category. It highlights significant variances and lets the commissaire drill into the underlying charges. Were overruns caused by emergency repairs, price increases, or scope changes? The data provides the context for an informed assessment.

The internal audit engine is a powerful aid for the commissaire aux comptes — not a replacement. It provides structured data, cross-references, and comparisons that help the commissaire move from number checking to informed evaluation. The generated report serves as a ready-made template the commissaire can use as a starting point for their own conclusions.

Important: Physical verification of supporting documents remains the commissaire’s responsibility. When the syndic attaches documents to transactions, the system flags their existence — but each document must still be verified individually by the commissaire. The tool ensures nothing is overlooked; the commissaire ensures everything is authentic.

AES-256-GCM Encryption

All data stored locally is encrypted with AES-256-GCM using PBKDF2 key derivation. This is the standard used by financial institutions and government agencies worldwide.

Integrity-Protected Documents

Owner portal HTML files use authenticated encryption. If anyone modifies any content — an amount, a name, a balance — the integrity check fails and the document displays a protection notice. Financial integrity is cryptographically guaranteed.

4-Level Access Control

Four distinct roles with granular permissions: Syndic (full access), Charges Assistant (budget & expenses), Payments Assistant (collections & statements), and Commissaire (audit-only).
